<p><p>The RFID tag chip market is primarily segmented into three types: HF (High Frequency) RFID chips operate at 13.56 MHz, commonly used for access control and smart cards due to their short-range capabilities. UHF (Ultra High Frequency) RFID chips function between 300 MHz and 3 GHz, enabling longer read ranges, making them ideal for supply chain management and asset tracking. LF (Low Frequency) RFID chips work at 125-134 kHz, often used for animal tracking and access management due to their robustness and reliability.</p></p>
