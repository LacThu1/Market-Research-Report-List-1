<p><p>The crop maintenance robot market comprises two main types: Unmanned Ground Robots (UGR) and Unmanned Aerial Robots (UAR). UGRs operate on the soil, performing tasks such as planting, weeding, and soil monitoring, offering precision and efficiency. Conversely, UARs utilize aerial capabilities for tasks like crop surveillance, mapping, and applying pesticides from above. Both types enhance agricultural productivity, reduce labor costs, and promote sustainable farming practices by leveraging advanced technologies for real-time data collection and automated operations.</p></p>
