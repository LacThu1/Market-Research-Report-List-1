<p><p>The single-cell sequencing market encompasses various techniques aimed at analyzing individual cells to uncover their unique genetic, transcriptomic, or epigenomic profiles. Single-cell isolation involves methods like microfluidics or FACS (fluorescence-activated cell sorting) to separate individual cells from a heterogeneous population. Single-cell amplification follows, enabling the replication of limited genetic material obtained from single cells to generate sufficient data for sequencing analysis. Together, these processes facilitate breakthroughs in personalized medicine, disease research, and developmental biology.</p></p>
