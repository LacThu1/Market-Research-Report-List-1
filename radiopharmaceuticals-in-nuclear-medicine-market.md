<p><p>Radiopharmaceuticals in nuclear medicine are categorized into three main types: diagnostic radiopharmaceuticals, therapy radiopharmaceuticals, and enriched stable isotopes. Diagnostic radiopharmaceuticals are used for imaging and identifying diseases, enabling visualization of physiological processes. Therapy radiopharmaceuticals deliver targeted radiation to treat conditions such as cancer, effectively destroying abnormal cells. Enriched stable isotopes, while not radioactive, are utilized in various applications like research and treatment, providing essential data and tools for advanced medical procedures. Together, these categories enhance patient diagnosis and treatment outcomes.</p></p>
