<p><p>Cataphoretic dip coatings, or electrocoating, involve the deposition of paint onto a substrate through electric current. The market is divided into two types: cathodic and anodic coatings. Cathodic coatings, which attract cations, offer superior corrosion resistance and durability, making them ideal for automotive and industrial applications. Anodic coatings, attracting anions, are typically used for decorative finishes due to their smooth appearance. Both types enhance surface protection and finish quality, catering to diverse industry requirements.</p></p>
