<p><p>Transmucosal drug delivery devices facilitate the administration of medications through mucosal membranes for rapid absorption. The buccal route uses the inner cheek for drug absorption, offering quick onset and bypassing the digestive system. The rectal route delivers drugs through the rectum, suitable for patients who are vomiting or unable to swallow. The nasal route utilizes the nasal cavity for systemic drug delivery, enabling rapid absorption due to the rich blood supply, making it effective for both local and systemic treatments.</p></p>
